## Loopback Address

- A loopback address is a special IP address that allows a device to communicate with itself..
- It is mainly used for testing and troubleshooting the TCP/IP stack.
- IPv4 Loopback Range: **127.0.0.0 – 127.255.255.255**
- The most commonly used loopback address is **127.0.0.1**, also known as **localhost**.
- Packets sent to a loopback address never leave the device; they are processed internally.

IPv4 Loopback Range
- 127.0.0.0 – 127.255.255.255
- The most commonly used loopback address is 127.0.0.1, also known as localhost.

IPv6 Loopback Address
